# v6.9 Scope Memo — FEP Boundary-Setting and v7 Bridge

**Status:** Reframed 2026-04-23 (second pass) against `paper-v6.8.2`. Supersedes the earlier "FEP Strengthening" framing.

## Intent revision

The original memo framed v6.9 as *FEP strengthening*. That was the wrong label. v6.8 already exposes more FEP than its estimators earn: §4.1 says governance "becomes free-energy monitoring" while §4.2 honestly hedges that full $-F$ is deferred. The gap between §4.1's framing and §4.2's estimator commitment is the cleanest reviewer attack vector in the paper.

v6.9's job is the opposite of strengthening. It is **boundary-setting**: reduce exposed FEP claims where they exceed what the estimators deliver, add targeted FEP-native vocabulary only where it is earned, and name v7's $\hat{F}$ spike as the explicit decision path for deeper FEP commitment. Net effect is defensive — protect v6.8 from reviewer attack, set up v7 as the horse race, do not deepen the FEP commitment before empirical validation earns it.

## What v6.8 already has

The drafting session should not re-propose any of this:

- **Correspondence table** (§4.1 "Choice of Frame"): Shannon entropy → S; MI → I; $-F$ → E; accumulated free-energy residual → V; KL/manifold distance → C.
- **Per-coordinate redefinitions** (§4.2): each coord has a formal info/FEP definition, a tiered computation recipe (Tier 1 = genuine variational/Shannon; Tier 2 = semantic proxy; Tier 3 = legacy heuristic), and a provenance tag.
- **V as free-energy debt**, not phenomenological void: $V_t = \lambda V_{t-1} + (1-\lambda)(F_t - F_{\text{ref}})$.
- **E as "precision / negative free energy"** is already the phrase used in §3.2.
- **FEP citations**: Friston 2010, Da Costa et al., Sajid et al., Smith/Friston/Whyte, Parr/Pezzulo/Friston.
- **Honest derivation-gap acknowledgment in §4.2**: full variational $-F$ deferred; resource-rate heuristic explicitly tagged "not equivalent to $-F$" and "does not approximate it under stationarity in any formal sense."

This is the substantive FEP integration. v6.9 does not add to it — v6.9 bounds it.

## Gaps — in priority order

### Gap 1: Soften §4.1 to match §4.2's estimator commitment

The current §4.1 wording is: FEP "gives a coherent top-level dynamic: agent drift corresponds to rising variational free energy, and governance becomes free-energy monitoring." This is stronger than §4.2 delivers. §4.2 ships provenance-tagged proxies with an explicit note that Tier-1 $-F$ instrumentation does not exist.

**What to write:** One paragraph rewrite in §4.1, ~100 words. Replace "governance becomes free-energy monitoring" with language like: "FEP supplies the target formalization; current deployment uses provenance-tagged proxies (§4.2) until Tier-1 generative-model instrumentation exists." Cross-reference the Tier structure in the same breath so the frame and the estimator commitment are read together.

**Payoff:** Eliminates the cleanest framing/implementation mismatch a reviewer can attack. Highest-priority v6.9 edit.

**Risk:** None. This is strictly reducing a claim the paper's own §4.2 already hedges.

### Gap 2: Active inference named as missing derivation (§15 Conclusion)

v6.8 defers full variational $-F$ to future work but does not explicitly name **active inference** as the framework that would close the loop between monitoring (passive observation of $F$) and governance (interventions that minimize expected free energy $G$). Verdicts (`guide`, `pause`, `reject`) are currently threshold-triggered; under active inference they would be recast as policy selections minimizing $G$ over future trajectories.

**What to write:** Short paragraph in §15, or a dedicated subsection "Toward Active-Inference Governance," ~300 words. State:
- Current verdicts = threshold-triggered, not derived from EFE minimization.
- A principled extension would replace CIRS v2's control law with an active-inference policy.
- This requires per-agent generative models — v7+ scope.
- Do **not** claim current verdicts are active-inference actions. They aren't.

**Risk:** Overclaiming. Anything that reads as "verdicts are active-inference policies" invites a Friston-literate reviewer to note the absent $G$ derivation.

### Gap 2a: Name the generative-model question as open (same paragraph as Gap 2)

§4.2's hedge on $E$ says full variational $-F$ is "deferred to subsequent work" because it "requires instrumentation that exposes the agent's generative model." That reads more tractable than it is. A Friston-literate reviewer will ask *which* generative model — the weights? a tokenized posterior over next-token distributions? the agent's implicit world-model as revealed through tool-use traces? For closed-weights frontier models, it is not obvious that $p(z, o)$ is well-defined at all in the strict variational sense.

**What to write:** One or two sentences inside the Gap 2 paragraph. Name this as an open methodological problem, not an engineering deferral. Prevents the "just instrument the model" misreading. Sets up v7's $\hat{F}$ spike (see `research/v7-fhat-spike-results.md`) as the explicit empirical decision path.

### Gap 3: Calibration ↔ precision bridge — softened wording

§9 (Class-Conditional Calibration) and §4 (E as precision / negative free energy) never talk to each other. Under FEP, calibration deviation is **evidence of precision miscalibration**, under the assumption that the agent's reported confidence tracks its inverse-variance weighting on prediction error. This is a softer claim than "calibration deviation is precision deviation" — the original memo wording was too strong.

**What to write:** 1–2 paragraph cross-reference in §9. Key moves:
- Use "evidence of" not "identical to."
- Name the assumption explicitly: *if* reported confidence proxies inverse-variance weighting, *then* calibration mismatch signals precision mismatch.
- Note this is a bridge, not an equivalence.

**Payoff:** Connects calibration protocol to active-inference precision-control literature without committing to a mapping that won't survive scrutiny.

**Risk:** If the wording is not careful, a reviewer will flag "reported confidence ≠ precision" and the bridge collapses. The assumption statement is load-bearing.

### Gap 4: Markov-blanket framing — optional, analogical only

v6.8 treats heterogeneous fleets as a class-structured population. The FEP-native framing is nested Markov blankets. If included, this must be explicitly **analogical, not a formal structural commitment**. Do not write "agent class = Markov blanket" in a way that sounds formal. Markov blankets attract controversy in FEP-critical circles (Biehl, Raja; "Markov blankets are over-claimed for biological systems"); the paper should not take on that fight for cosmetic gain.

**What to write:** At most one short paragraph in §2, ~150 words, hedged as structural scaffolding vocabulary. Explicit caveat: "we use Markov-blanket vocabulary as scaffolding, not as an ontological claim."

**Drop rule:** If the drafter is uncertain whether the paragraph pulls weight, drop it. The paper is coherent without it.

### Removed / dropped gaps

**~~Coherence as model evidence~~ — REMOVED.** An earlier memo draft proposed labeling $C = \exp(-D_{\mathrm{KL}}(q_{\text{now}} \| q_{\text{ref}}))$ as "Bayesian model evidence." This is technically wrong. Marginal likelihood $p(D \mid M)$ integrates over model parameters; $\exp(-D_{\mathrm{KL}})$ is a divergence-based similarity to a reference distribution. The one-liner would invite a technical objection. Do not add it.

**~~Resource-rate proxy correlation with $-F$~~ — DROPPED.** No throughput-to-$-F$ bridge without a generative model, which Gap 2a just named as ill-defined for closed-weights LLMs. Writing a stationarity claim would either overclaim or recurse into Gap 2a. §4.2's current "not equivalent to $-F$, does not approximate it under stationarity in any formal sense" is the honest stopping point.

## Positioning note for the drafter

v6.9 is defensive, not offensive. Its job is to protect v6.8 from reviewer attack while setting up v7's $\hat{F}$ spike as the explicit decision path. The bar for every v6.9 change is: does this *reduce* exposed claims, or does it merely relabel? If reduce, keep. If relabel, keep only if the relabeling is surgical and unassailable. Any claim that could invite a technical objection gets dropped.

The horse race — does $\hat{F}$ add predictive value over EISV proxies? — is where FEP earns deeper commitment. The paper narrative does not.
